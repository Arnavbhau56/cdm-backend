1. Call Notes

Overview

Sandowitch is a sandwich-focused QSR brand being built as a single-category, value-led, grab-and-go format with
a tightly controlled menu and centralized production model. The core thesis is that sandwiches are a large but
under-branded category in India: they cut across breakfast, snack, and meal occasions, are culturally familiar, and
have room for a more scaled brand between low-priced local players and premium gourmet offerings. The business
is currently live in Bangalore with one cloud kitchen and one retail store, after spending 6 months testing delivery
and product travel from the central kitchen before opening the first store in November. The model is built around a
central kitchen plus spoke stores with no live cooking at the store level beyond assembly, which keeps store
complexity low and supports future franchising. Sandowitch is currently raising a pre-seed round to fund store
rollout, with founder Vivek Prabhakar bringing deep consumer brand-building experience from Chumbak and prior
brand / retail experience of over 30 years.

Problem

Sandwiches are a widely consumed category in India, but no scaled national sandwich-first brand has
been built yet.
The current market is split between:

low-priced local/unorganized sandwich stores
Subway-like formats at higher price points
gourmet sandwich players priced at ₹500–600+

The founder sees whitespace in the ₹180–250 accessible value band.
Existing QSR formats often hit limits because they are tied to a narrow occasion:

dessert-led brands
snack-led brands
beverage-led formats

The founder's belief is that sandwiches are stronger because they work across occasions:

3.  Internal Notes

Sando

Burgers have McDonald's. Pizza has Domino's. Biryani has multiple scaled plays. But sandwiches, the world's

most ever-present convenience meal, remain a white space in branded Indian QSR. 

Subway exists, but operates an old playbook with cold subs, a franchise-heavy model, and no real cultural

resonance with Indian. Sandowitch is India's first flavour-forward, personality-packed sandwich QSR — Indian in

soul, global in format, built for the next decade of urban consumption.

Sector Tailwinds

1. White space 

No Indian-born, scaled sandwich QSR exists. Subway is the only credible player — and it's a foreign brand with a

format that hasn't evolved for India. This is Domino's opportunity for sandwiches. 

2. Haldiram's is in advanced talks to bring Jimmy John's (2,600 US outlets, $2.6B system sales) to India to compete

with Subway. Smart money is recognising this category.  (https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/business/india-

3. Comparable: A sandwizza won't be the right comparable, but Subway India COCO 389crs revenue (433 stores),

store EBITDA: 14.8%, and Sandowitch at ₹2.5 Cr ARR in month 3.

4. Central Kitchen → 10–12 QSR outlets model mirrors the playbook of Wow! Momo and Biggie's Burger —

India's most successfully scaled homegrown QSR formats. Capex at ₹70–75L/store is lean and franchise-ready. 

5. Vibrant diner-aesthetic store design, Instagrammable food, sub-₹200 entry point, and a personality-first identity.

63% dine-in orders signal real destination pull — not just delivery dependency. 

6. India's QSR market hits $43.5B by 2030. Cloud kitchens and QSRs will grow 40% faster than overall F&B. Tier

1 cities are already saturated for burgers/pizza — sandwiches could be next

USP / MOAT / Defensibility 

1. In-house formulation

All bread is baked in-house at the Central Kitchen, and sauces and recipes are also in-house. This is the single

biggest quality differentiator vs. Subway's frozen bun supply chain — and creates a defensible taste moat with a

possible FMCG play in the roadmap in the future. 

2. Hot & Toasty Format

Indians prefer hot food. Sandowitch's toasty sandwich format aligns with Indian eating habits — unlike Subway's

cold-sub model, which requires consumer behaviour change.

3. Menu Discipline

Lean, tight menu: 14 sandwiches, sides, salads, desserts, drinks. Top 3 sandwiches = 40% of sales — a clear signal

of operational focus and repeat purchase patterns.

4. Pricing

Entry at ₹149. Core sandwiches at ₹179–₹249. Full meal under ₹400. This is the sweet spot for India's urban

working class and students — the highest frequency QSR cohort.

5. Diner Aesthetic / Instagrammable

American-Japanese diner inspiration, vibrant murals, bold brand identity. The store is a social media asset —

driving organic discovery at zero CAC.

6. Central Kitchen Infrastructure

One CK serves 10–12 outlets + 1 cloud kitchen. This mirrors Wow! Momo's exact scale model — keeping quality

consistent while keeping costs low across expansion.
